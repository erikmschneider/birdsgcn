The SGCN Bird Atlas

Every U.S. state and territory names the wildlife it considers most in need of conservation. This maps the birds — which Species of Greatest Conservation Need each state and territory lists, and where any one bird turns up across the country.

About the data. Bird taxa each state or territory designated as Species of Greatest Conservation Need (SGCN) in its State Wildlife Action Plan, compiled by the USGS from USFWS records. The year shown for each state or territory is its most recent plan revision in this dataset. A state or territory “listing” a bird means that taxon appears in its SWAP.

Groups are an approximate convenience grouping derived from common names, not a formal taxonomy. Alaska, Hawaii, and the five territories are drawn to their own geometry; the Pacific and Caribbean island endemics appear in the territory tiles below the map.
